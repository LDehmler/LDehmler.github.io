About

Bicycling Monterey provides resources and inspiration for people who bike anywhere and serves as an Information Hub for Biking Monterey County. A grassroots volunteer effort founded 2009, Bicycling Monterey is local partner of the California Bicycle Coalition. Below is an overview about the Bicycling Monterey site and projects, and a sampling of our photos of visitors and residents biking in Monterey County.


See what people say about the Bicycling Monterey work. Bicycling Monterey is renowned for accurate, reliable information. This website features over 700 web pages of original content, including thousands of original photos. Bicycling Monterey includes numerous bike advocacy projects.

Why we do what we do: Bicycling helps reduce the environmental impact of transportation, including carbon emissions. Bicycling improves public health, strengthens communities, and has multiple other benefits. Besides, biking is fun!

Bicycling Monterey is a local partner of the California Bicycle Coalition and collaborates on local, regional, state, national, and international bicycling advocacy efforts.

As of the start of 2023, there is not yet an official bicycling coalition for Monterey County, or for the Monterey Bay Tri-County Region. Bicycling Monterey helps fill that gap, including with its 35-section directory of Monterey County Bicycling Resources / Bike Community Leaders.

How else does a place become more welcoming for people who bike? See “Helping visitors, and residents, use sustainable transportation.”


Valued by a wide mix of people, Bicycling Monterey is a trusted resource linked by local, regional, state, and national organizations, e.g., the Transportation Agency for Monterey County, Cruz511 – Santa Cruz County Regional Transportation Commission, University of California at Berkeley’s CATSIP, League of American Bicyclists, People for Bikes, and many others.


Since founded in 2009, the work of the founder on this website and on all Bicycling Monterey projects has been done as an unpaid volunteer. That’s still the case at the start of 2023.
